Nursery Science - Chapter 4: Materials Around Us

TOPIC 1: Materials

Introduction:
Exploring different materials and their names.

Activities:
1. Identifying wood, plastic, metal, fabric, paper
2. Material hunts - finding things made of each material
3. Sorting objects by what they're made from
4. Feeling different materials
5. Talking about uses of different materials

Key Vocabulary:
material, wood, plastic, metal, fabric, paper, glass, made from

Resources Needed:
- Material samples
- Objects made from different materials
- Sorting trays
- Magnifying glasses
- Picture cards

Assessment Checkpoint:
Can name some common materials? Sorts objects by material? Shows curiosity about materials?

---

TOPIC 2: Properties

Introduction:
Investigating how materials feel and behave.

Activities:
1. Testing soft vs hard objects
2. Finding rough and smooth textures
3. Exploring bendy and stiff materials
4. Transparent and opaque investigations
5. Heavy and light comparisons

Key Vocabulary:
soft, hard, rough, smooth, bendy, stiff, heavy, light, see-through

Resources Needed:
- Variety of objects
- Feely bags
- Balance scales
- Clear and opaque materials
- Testing equipment

Assessment Checkpoint:
Can describe properties using simple words? Compares materials? Tests properties through play?

---

TOPIC 3: Waterplay Investigations

Introduction:
Discovering properties of water and floating/sinking.

Activities:
1. Pouring and filling activities
2. Testing what floats and what sinks
3. Mixing water with different materials
4. Observing ice melting
5. Making things dissolve (sugar, salt)

Key Vocabulary:
water, wet, dry, float, sink, pour, splash, melt, dissolve, drip

Resources Needed:
- Water tray
- Containers
- Objects to test
- Ice cubes
- Dissolving materials
- Towels

Assessment Checkpoint:
Explores water properties? Predicts floating/sinking? Observes changes in water play?

---

TOPIC 4: Changing Materials

Introduction:
Observing simple changes in materials.

Activities:
1. Play dough - squashing, rolling, changing shape
2. Cooking activities - seeing changes (melting, mixing)
3. Ice to water experiments
4. Mixing colors - paint, playdough
5. Tearing, cutting, molding materials

Key Vocabulary:
change, different, same, melt, freeze, mix, squash, stretch, tear, cut

Resources Needed:
- Playdough
- Cooking ingredients
- Ice cubes
- Art materials
- Moldable materials

Assessment Checkpoint:
Notices when materials change? Can describe changes? Explores changing materials safely?
