Year 1 English - Cambridge Primary Curriculum
TOPIC 3: Reading Comprehension

Theory: Good readers make sense of what they read by thinking about characters, events, and meanings. They can answer questions and make predictions.

Reading Strategies:
- Sound out unfamiliar words
- Use picture clues
- Re-read if confused
- Think about what makes sense
- Predict what happens next
- Visualize the story

Comprehension Questions:
- Who? (characters)
- What? (events)
- Where? (setting)
- When? (time)
- Why? (reasons)
- How? (method)

Example Text: "Jack climbed the tall beanstalk. He saw a giant's castle in the clouds. Jack felt scared but he was brave."

Questions:
- Who climbed the beanstalk? (Jack)
- What did Jack see? (A giant's castle)
- How did Jack feel? (Scared but brave)
- Why do you think Jack was brave? (He kept going even though he was scared)

Worksheets:
1. Read and Answer: Read a short story and answer 5 questions
2. True or False: Mark statements about the text as T or F
3. Story Sequence: Put events in order (1, 2, 3, 4)
4. Character Feelings: How did the character feel at the start? At the end?
