Year 2 English - Cambridge Primary Curriculum
TOPIC 5: Story Writing with Structure

Theory: Well-structured stories have clear beginning, middle, and end with developed characters and settings.

Story Mountain:
1. Opening: introduce characters and setting
2. Build-up: something starts to happen
3. Problem/Climax: main event or difficulty
4. Resolution: problem solved
5. Ending: story concludes

Story Elements:
- Characters: names, appearance, personality, feelings
- Setting: where (place description), when (time)
- Plot: what happens, in logical order
- Problem: what goes wrong
- Solution: how it's fixed
- Ending: how characters feel, what they learned

Narrative Language:
- Time connectives: First, Then, Next, After that, Finally, Suddenly
- Description: adjectives, similes, senses
- Speech: "Help me!" shouted Tom.
- Show don't tell: trembling hands (not "he was scared")

Worksheets:
1. Story Plan: Complete story mountain for your own story
2. Character Profile: Create detailed character description
3. Setting Description: Describe a magical forest using all five senses
4. Finish the Story: Complete a story from given opening
