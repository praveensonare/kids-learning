Year 6 Science - Cambridge Primary Standards (Preparation for Secondary)

Learning Objectives: Take measurements with increasing accuracy, record data using scientific diagrams/labels/tables, identify scientific evidence to support/refute ideas, report findings from enquiries

Key Topics:
TOPIC 1: Living Things/Classification: Classification of living things (kingdoms, vertebrates/invertebrates); give reasons for classification based on characteristics; Carl Linnaeus
TOPIC 2: Animals/Humans: Circulatory system (heart, blood vessels, blood); impact of diet, exercise, drugs, lifestyle on body function; nutrients and water transported in blood; heart pumps blood
TOPIC 3: Evolution/Inheritance: Characteristics inherited from parents; variation in offspring; adaptation leads to evolution; fossils provide evidence; Charles Darwin, Alfred Wallace, Mary Anning
TOPIC 4: Light: Light travels in straight lines; objects seen when light reflects into eye; light travels from source to eye or source to object to eye; shadows have same shape as objects
TOPIC 5: Electricity: Associate brightness of bulb with number/voltage of cells; compare/give reasons for variations in components; use recognized symbols for circuits (cell, battery, bulb, switch, buzzer, motor)
Worksheets: Classification keys, circulatory system diagrams, inheritance patterns, light ray diagrams, circuit drawings with symbols
