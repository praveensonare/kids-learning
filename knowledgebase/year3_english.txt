Year 3 English - Cambridge Primary Curriculum

Learning Objectives:
- Read a range of fiction, non-fiction, and poetry
- Use dictionaries to check meanings
- Write narratives with detailed settings and characters
- Use conjunctions, adverbs, and prepositions
- Organize paragraphs around a theme
- Present ideas orally with expression

Key Topics:

TOPIC 1: Reading Comprehension
Theory: Year 3 readers analyze texts deeply, making inferences and connections.

Skills:
- Predicting from details
- Making inferences about characters' motives
- Identifying themes and conventions
- Retrieving and recording information
- Discussing words and phrases that capture imagination
- Understanding non-fiction structures (contents, index, headings)

TOPIC 2: Writing - Narrative
Story Elements:
- Settings: describe time and place vividly
- Characters: develop personalities, feelings, motivations  
- Plot: clear structure with problem and resolution
- Dialogue: punctuated speech to show character

Techniques:
- Show don't tell: actions reveal feelings
- Varied sentence openers: -ly words, -ing words, time phrases
- Descriptive language: powerful verbs, precise adjectives
- Paragraphs: new speaker, new time, new place, new idea

TOPIC 3: Grammar and Punctuation
- Present perfect: have/has + past participle (I have eaten)
- Conjunctions: when, before, after, while, because, if
- Adverbs: quickly, carefully, yesterday, soon
- Prepositions: above, below, beside, through, during
- Inverted commas: "speech marks" for dialogue
- Apostrophes: possession (girl's, girls')
- Paragraphs: group related sentences

TOPIC 4: Spelling and Vocabulary
Prefixes and suffixes:
- un-, dis-, mis-, re-, pre-, sub- (unhappy, disappear, mistake)
- -ly, -ation, -ous, -sure, -ture (quickly, creation, famous)
- Word families: help - helpful - helpless - unhelpful
- Homophones: there/their/they're, to/too/two, hear/here

TOPIC 5: Poetry
Forms:
- Haiku: 5-7-5 syllables
- Kennings: two-word descriptions (wave-rider = ship)  
- Free verse: no rhyme scheme
- Performance poetry: rhythm and repetition

Devices:
- Similes and metaphors
- Personification: giving human qualities to objects
- Alliteration and onomatopoeia
- Imagery: painting pictures with words

Worksheets and Activities:
1. Character profiles with evidence from text
2. Write dialogue between characters
3. Create story mountain plans
4. Use adverbs and conjunctions correctly
5. Spell words with prefixes and suffixes
6. Analyze and write poetry
7. Edit and improve writing
8. Present stories to an audience
