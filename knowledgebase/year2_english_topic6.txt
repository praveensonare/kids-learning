Year 2 English - Cambridge Primary Curriculum
TOPIC 6: Non-Fiction Writing

Theory: Non-fiction texts inform, instruct, or explain. They have different structures from stories.

Non-Fiction Text Types:
- Instructions: How to make/do something (recipe, game rules)
- Information: Facts about a topic (animal fact file, country information)
- Recount: Retell events that happened (diary, news report)
- Explanation: How or why something works (life cycle, how rainbow forms)

Features of Instructions:
- Title: What you're making/doing
- List: Equipment or ingredients needed
- Steps: Numbered, in order, start with verb (Cut, Mix, Pour)
- Time connectives: First, Next, Then, Finally
- Clear, simple language

Features of Information Texts:
- Heading: Topic name
- Introduction: What text is about
- Sub-headings: Organize different sections
- Facts: True information
- Diagrams: Labeled pictures
- Captions: Explain pictures

Worksheets:
1. Write Instructions: How to make a sandwich
2. Fact File: Create information page about favorite animal
3. Diary Entry: Recount your weekend
4. Label Diagram: Annotate picture with facts
