Nursery Literacy - Chapter 4: Stories and Rhymes

TOPIC 1: Story Time

Introduction:
Developing a love for stories and books through interactive reading.

Activities:
1. Daily story time with picture books
2. Acting out favorite stories
3. Talking about story characters and events
4. Looking at pictures and predicting what happens next
5. Retelling familiar stories with props

Key Vocabulary:
story, book, character, beginning, middle, end, favorite, once upon a time

Resources Needed:
- Picture books
- Story props
- Puppets
- Story sacks

Assessment Checkpoint:
Can sit and listen to simple stories? Shows interest in books? Can talk about story?

---

TOPIC 2: Nursery Rhymes

Introduction:
Learning traditional nursery rhymes and action songs.

Activities:
1. Singing Twinkle Twinkle, Baa Baa Black Sheep, Humpty Dumpty
2. Learning actions to go with rhymes
3. Filling in missing words in familiar rhymes
4. Clapping rhythm patterns
5. Creating own versions of rhymes

Key Vocabulary:
rhyme, song, words, rhythm, repeat, verse, together

Resources Needed:
- Nursery rhyme books
- Props for rhymes
- Musical instruments
- Rhyme cards

Assessment Checkpoint:
Can join in with familiar rhymes? Remembers some words? Enjoys rhythm activities?

---

TOPIC 3: Story Sequencing

Introduction:
Understanding that stories have a beginning, middle, and end.

Activities:
1. Arranging story picture cards in order
2. Talking about what happened first, next, last
3. Drawing pictures from a story in sequence
4. Using time words (first, then, next, finally)
5. Creating own simple sequences

Key Vocabulary:
first, next, then, after, last, beginning, middle, end, order

Resources Needed:
- Sequencing cards
- Story pictures
- Drawing materials
- Simple story books

Assessment Checkpoint:
Can sequence 3 simple pictures? Uses time language? Understands story order?

---

TOPIC 4: Role Play

Introduction:
Using imagination and language in pretend play.

Activities:
1. Home corner role play (cooking, shopping, caring for babies)
2. Dressing up and pretending to be different characters
3. Acting out familiar stories
4. Creating imaginary scenarios
5. Using props to support pretend play

Key Vocabulary:
pretend, imagine, let's pretend, role, character, acting, make-believe

Resources Needed:
- Dressing up clothes
- Home corner equipment
- Dolls and teddies
- Play food and props

Assessment Checkpoint:
Engages in imaginative play? Uses language in role play? Stays in role?
