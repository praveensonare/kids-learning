Nursery Science - Chapter 2: Plants and Animals

TOPIC 1: Living Things

Introduction:
Understanding what makes something alive.

Activities:
1. Sorting living and non-living things
2. Observing movements of living things
3. Talking about eating, growing, moving
4. Caring for classroom plants and pets
5. Looking for signs of life outdoors

Key Vocabulary:
alive, living, grow, eat, move, breathe, animal, plant

Resources Needed:
- Picture cards
- Classroom plants
- Small pets/creatures
- Sorting hoops
- Nature area

Assessment Checkpoint:
Beginning to understand living vs non-living? Can name some living things?

---

TOPIC 2: Plants

Introduction:
Learning about plants and how they grow.

Activities:
1. Planting seeds and watching them grow
2. Looking at different parts of plants (roots, stem, leaves, flower)
3. Watering and caring for plants
4. Comparing different types of plants
5. Observing seasonal changes in plants

Key Vocabulary:
plant, seed, grow, roots, stem, leaf, flower, water, soil, sun

Resources Needed:
- Seeds and pots
- Watering cans
- Real plants
- Picture books about plants
- Magnifying glasses

Assessment Checkpoint:
Can name parts of a plant? Understands plants need water? Shows care for plants?

---

TOPIC 3: Animals

Introduction:
Exploring different animals and their characteristics.

Activities:
1. Learning about pets (dog, cat, rabbit, fish)
2. Farm animals and their sounds
3. Wild animals - where do they live?
4. Animal movement games (hop like a rabbit, waddle like a duck)
5. Sorting animals by habitat

Key Vocabulary:
animal, pet, wild, farm, fur, feathers, scales, baby, home, habitat

Resources Needed:
- Toy animals
- Animal picture cards
- Animal story books
- Videos of animals
- Animal masks

Assessment Checkpoint:
Can name several animals? Knows some animal sounds? Shows interest in animals?

---

TOPIC 4: Growth

Introduction:
Understanding that living things grow and change.

Activities:
1. Looking at baby and grown-up pictures
2. Measuring height - I'm growing!
3. Observing tadpoles or caterpillars changing
4. Looking at baby animals and adult animals
5. Timeline of growing from baby to child

Key Vocabulary:
grow, baby, big, change, younger, older, taller, bigger

Resources Needed:
- Growth charts
- Baby/adult animal pictures
- Family photos
- Lifecycle models
- Measuring equipment

Assessment Checkpoint:
Understands that living things grow? Can talk about growing bigger? Observes changes?
