Reception English - Cambridge Primary Curriculum
TOPIC 6: Story Comprehension

Theory: Comprehension means understanding what we read or hear. Good readers think about characters, settings, and events.

Story Elements:
- Characters: Who is in the story?
- Setting: Where and when does it happen?
- Beginning: How does the story start?
- Middle: What happens?
- End: How does it end?
- Problem and Solution: What goes wrong? How is it fixed?

Examples - "The Three Billy Goats Gruff":
- Characters: 3 goats, troll
- Setting: Bridge over a river
- Problem: Troll won't let goats cross
- Solution: Biggest goat pushes troll into water

Worksheets:
1. Story Map: Draw the beginning, middle, and end of "Goldilocks"
2. Character Questions: Who was kind? Who was naughty?
3. Prediction: What do you think will happen next?
4. Retelling: Put the story pictures in order 1, 2, 3, 4
