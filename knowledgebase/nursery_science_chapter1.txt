Nursery Science - Chapter 1: My Five Senses

TOPIC 1: Sight

Introduction:
Exploring how we use our eyes to see the world.

Activities:
1. Looking at colorful objects and pictures
2. Using magnifying glasses to examine things closely
3. Color sorting and matching games
4. Looking for specific colors on nature walks
5. Dark tent experiences - what happens with no light?

Key Vocabulary:
see, look, eyes, color, bright, dark, watch, observe

Resources Needed:
- Magnifying glasses
- Colorful objects
- Picture books
- Flashlights
- Color cards

Assessment Checkpoint:
Can identify that we see with eyes? Names some colors? Describes what they see?

---

TOPIC 2: Hearing

Introduction:
Discovering sounds and how we hear with our ears.

Activities:
1. Sound hunts - listening for different sounds
2. Loud and quiet sound comparisons
3. Guessing sounds game (what made that noise?)
4. Making sounds with instruments
5. Listening to environmental sounds (rain, birds, traffic)

Key Vocabulary:
hear, listen, sound, loud, quiet, noisy, ears, music

Resources Needed:
- Musical instruments
- Sound recording devices
- Listening props
- Story tapes/CDs

Assessment Checkpoint:
Understands we hear with ears? Can describe sounds as loud/quiet? Listens attentively?

---

TOPIC 3: Touch

Introduction:
Feeling different textures and properties through touch.

Activities:
1. Feely bag activities - guessing objects by touch
2. Texture walks - feeling rough bark, smooth leaves
3. Sorting soft and hard objects
4. Playing with different materials (sand, water, playdough)
5. Creating texture collages

Key Vocabulary:
touch, feel, soft, hard, rough, smooth, bumpy, warm, cold

Resources Needed:
- Feely bags
- Textured materials
- Natural objects
- Sensory play materials

Assessment Checkpoint:
Uses touch to explore objects? Can describe simple textures? Knows we feel with hands/skin?

---

TOPIC 4: Taste and Smell

Introduction:
Exploring how we taste and smell different things.

Activities:
1. Safe tasting activities - sweet, salty, sour
2. Smell jars - guessing scents (orange, mint, coffee)
3. Sorting foods we like and don't like
4. Cooking activities involving smelling ingredients
5. Flower and herb sniffing activities

Key Vocabulary:
taste, smell, tongue, nose, sweet, sour, yummy, yucky, sniff

Resources Needed:
- Safe tasting foods
- Smell jars
- Fresh herbs
- Cooking ingredients
- Picture cards

Assessment Checkpoint:
Knows we taste with tongue and smell with nose? Can describe some tastes/smells?
