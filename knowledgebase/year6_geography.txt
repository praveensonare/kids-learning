Year 6 Geography - Cambridge Primary Standards (Preparation for Secondary)

Learning Objectives: Locate/describe world features, understand physical/human processes, interpret sources including maps/graphs/GIS, use fieldwork

Key Topics:
TOPIC 1: Global Geography: All continents; major countries; lines of latitude/longitude; Equator, Tropics of Cancer/Capricorn, Arctic/Antarctic Circles; time zones; global climate patterns
TOPIC 2: Physical Geography Processes: Mountains (fold, volcanic, fault-block); rivers (erosion, deposition, flood plains); coasts (erosion, weathering, deposition, longshore drift); earthquakes and volcanoes (plate tectonics)
TOPIC 3: Human Geography: Population distribution and density; urbanization; megacities; migration (push/pull factors); settlements; economic activities; trade links; resources (renewable, non-renewable)
TOPIC 4: Regional Study: In-depth study of region (e.g., Russia, Arctic, Middle East, Asia); physical features; climate; people and culture; economy; connections with other regions
TOPIC 5: Geographical Skills: OS maps; contour lines; six-figure grid references; scales and distances; fieldwork techniques; data presentation (graphs, GIS); aerial photos and satellite images
TOPIC 6: Sustainability and Conservation: Climate change causes/effects; carbon footprint; renewable energy; sustainable cities; conservation efforts; individual actions
Worksheets: World map challenges, physical process diagrams, population graphs, fieldwork planning, GIS activities, sustainability projects
